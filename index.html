Federally, this is a "security + affordability" session. The Carney government is pushing national-security and law-and-order bills — borders, surveillance, hate crime, bail — alongside cost-of-living measures. The security bills are where the rights fights are. The biggest is the Strong Borders Act (C-2), which after a huge backlash was split into C-12 (immigration/border — now law) and C-22 (the "lawful access" surveillance powers — in committee and being rushed).

In the provinces, each legislature has its own fights — resource development, health care, housing, privacy/transparency, and policing. Use the Provinces & Territories section to jump to yours — all ten provinces and three territories.

🚩 Red-flag bills — the ones to actually read

🛑

Before you share a scary "bill number," check it.

Bill numbers get recycled every Parliament, and viral posts pin alarming claims on the wrong one. Example: "Bill C-206" is passed around as sinister — but in this Parliament C-206 is the National Strategy on Brain Injuries Act, a harmless health bill. The genuinely alarming ones are C-2/C-12/C-22, C-8, C-9, C-14, and C-34. Confirm any number on LEGISinfo first.

🧭 Your rights, by theme

The cross-cutting map — what could be eroded, beyond just privacy.

🔐 Privacy & data

Surveillance/lawful access (C-22, C-8), age-verification → de-facto digital ID (C-34), PIPEDA rewrite (C-36). Provincially: freedom-of-information rewrites, traffic cameras, voter-roll expansion.

🗣️ Free expression

Hate-speech definitions (C-9), gating online speech behind ID and weakening anonymity (C-34), third-party political-advocacy rules (C-25).

✊ Protest & assembly

Labour and faith groups warn C-9's wording could touch peaceful protest; several provinces have moved on protest/injunction and "critical infrastructure" rules.

⚖️ Due process

Reverse-onus bail and more pre-trial detention of legally-innocent people (C-14); the one-year asylum-hearing bar (C-12).

🔍 Search & surveillance

Warrantless / low-threshold data demands and encryption pressure (C-2/C-22), secret cyber orders (C-8), new financial-crime police (C-29), automated traffic cameras.

🛂 Immigration & refugees

One-year asylum bar, broad ministerial power to cancel status, expanded data-sharing (C-12) — flagged by the Canadian Council for Refugees.

🪶 Indigenous rights

Consultation/UNDRIP concerns on fast-tracked projects (C-5, and several provincial "build faster" / resource bills).

🏛️ Accountability

Watchdog independence, freedom-of-information limits, and cabinet secrecy are recurring provincial battlegrounds — see your province below.
