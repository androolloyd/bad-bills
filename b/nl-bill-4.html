Bill 4LOWER CONCERN

Revenue Administration Act (Amendment) — repeal of the sugar-sweetened beverage tax

Sponsor: Hon. Craig Pardy, Minister of Finance (PC) · Status: Royal Assent (June 2, 2026); the tax was administratively removed effective July 1, 2025 and this Act repeals the underlying framework · Newfoundland and Labrador · Provincial

Formally repeals the 'sugar tax' (the 20-cents-per-litre Sugar-Sweetened Beverage Tax on pop, sweetened juices, slushies and non-diet fountain drinks introduced in 2022) by striking Part IX.2 of the Act, plus technical tax-definition cleanups.

✅ The case for

Removes a regressive, broadly unpopular levy (~$9M/year) the government argues hit lower-income households hardest; a flagged affordability/cost-of-living win. Government says programs the tax had funded (school food, glucose-monitoring pilot, physical-activity credit) continue from general revenue.

⚠️ The case against

Public-health groups (Heart & Stroke and others) opposed the repeal, arguing such taxes reduce sugary-drink consumption and that 'walking back the pop tax comes at a cost' in foregone revenue and lost health benefit. Adds to revenue pressure during a deficit.

🔐 Privacy & civil liberties: Minimal.

👥 Who it affects

All consumers of sugary drinks (relief), retailers, and provincial revenues/health-promotion programs.

✔️ Fact-checkVerified: Bill 4 (An Act to Amend the Revenue Administration Act) repeals the sugar-sweetened beverage tax; it passed third reading May 4, 2026 and received Royal Assent June 2, 2026 (SNL c.3). Clarification on timing/misinformation: the tax (Canada's first such provincial tax, in effect since Sept. 1, 2022) was effectively eliminated July 1, 2025 by ministerial action after Premier John Hogan's announcement, and Bill 4 is the statutory clean-up that formally strikes Part IX.2 from the Act — so the levy was already gone before this bill passed. Health officials confirmed scrapping it would not end the related glucose-monitoring/diabetes programs.
