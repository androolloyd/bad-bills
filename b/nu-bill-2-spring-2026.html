Bill 2 (Spring 2026)LOWER CONCERN

Supplementary Appropriation (Capital) Act, No. 1, 2026-2027

Sponsor: Premier John Main, Minister of Finance · Status: PASSED - Royal assent June 11, 2026. · Nunavut · Provincial

Authorizes approximately $273.9 million in additional capital spending for the 2026-27 fiscal year on projects and purchases across about 10 Government of Nunavut departments. The largest single share (~$123M) goes to the Department of Transportation and Infrastructure. It supplements the $373.2M capital plan approved during the March winter sitting and funds infrastructure such as roads, buildings, water/wastewater systems and community facilities. Part of the overall ~$3.7B 2026-27 spending package.

✅ The case for

Funds badly needed northern infrastructure (transportation links, water/wastewater, community facilities) in communities with severe infrastructure deficits and no road connections between them; capital investment supports housing, health and education facility construction.

⚠️ The case against

Nunavut has a long record of capital under-spending and project delays due to short construction seasons, sealift logistics and labour/contractor shortages, so appropriated capital does not always translate into delivered projects; heavy reliance on federal funding; limited transparency on project-by-project delivery timelines.

🔐 Privacy & civil liberties: Minimal. A capital appropriation authorizes spending on physical assets and creates no data-collection or civil-liberties powers.

👥 Who it affects

Communities receiving infrastructure (water, transport, buildings); GN departments delivering capital projects; northern construction sector; all residents who rely on public infrastructure.
