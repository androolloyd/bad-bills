Bill 5HIGH CONCERN

Act to accelerate the authorizations required to carry out priority projects of national scope ('Q-5' megaproject fast-track)

Status: DIED on the order paper (mort au feuilleton) at the end of session, June 2026 — not adopted. The CAQ says it will reintroduce it in the fall if re-elected. · Quebec · Provincial

Sponsored by Finance Minister Eric Girard, it would have created a single, expedited authorization track for projects the Finance Minister designates 'priority/national scope' (e.g., large infrastructure, the battery/EV supply chain), letting Cabinet modify or suspend Quebec laws — including environmental statutes — for those projects.

✅ The case for

Government framed it as boosting the economy, jobs and Quebec's 'autonomy' and resilience, cutting red tape, and speeding strategic projects while nominally respecting environmental review, public health/safety and Indigenous rights, with collaboration among ministries, municipalities and Indigenous communities.

⚠️ The case against

The Quebec Environmental Law Centre (CQDE), Nature Québec, the Centre québécois du droit de l'environnement and the Fédération québécoise des municipalités warned it handed Cabinet sweeping power to bypass environmental law with no expiry on exemptions ('regulatory free passes for up to 100 years'), gave the Environment Ministry no added funding or inspection capacity, and cut public-consultation windows from 45 to 20 days, making meaningful local input nearly impossible. Most opposition safeguard amendments were rejected; QS 'obstruction' was blamed by the government for the bill's death.

🔐 Privacy & civil liberties: Privacy: minimal. The serious concerns are democratic accountability, environmental protection, and Indigenous consultation/consent — concentrating discretionary power in Cabinet and shrinking public participation and oversight.

👥 Who it affects

Communities near major projects, environmental and Indigenous groups, municipalities, project proponents/industry, and the Environment Ministry.

✔️ Fact-checkVerified: Bill 5 (An Act to accelerate the granting of the authorizations required to carry out priority national-scale projects), the 'Q-5' megaproject fast-track, was introduced December 9, 2025 by Finance Minister Eric Girard and reinstated in the third session on May 6, 2026. It lets the government designate projects as 'priority and of national scope' for five years and issue a single government authorization replacing permits otherwise required under laws listed in Schedule I (with conditions; an authorization lapses if a project has not begun within two years). Correction/caveat: explicitly modelled on federal Bill C-5 and Ontario's Bill 5, but as of mid-June 2026 it had reached committee study and was among the major bills the CAQ was trying to push before the session closed June 12 - it is NOT confirmed to have received final adoption, so it should not be described as already law.
