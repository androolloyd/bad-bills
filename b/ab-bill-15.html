Public Safety and Emergency Services Statutes Amendment Act, 2026 (Alberta Sheriffs Police Service)

Sponsor: Minister of Public Safety and Emergency Services (UCP) · Status: Received Royal Assent April 16, 2026. Implementation underway (transition of ~1,200 sheriff staff to the new Crown corporation). · Alberta · Provincial

Lays the legal groundwork to transition Alberta sheriffs into a new independent provincial police body, the Alberta Sheriffs Police Service (ASPS), structured as an arms-length Crown corporation. Provides a path for eligible sheriff peace officers to become full ASPS police officers (with additional training), moves ~1,200 staff and their budget, and affirms officers' right to form an association for collective bargaining.

✅ The case for

Government frames it as a modern, accountable, made-in-Alberta police capability; protects affected employees' jobs and bargaining rights; supporters see faster, more locally responsive policing and a step toward provincial autonomy in law enforcement.

⚠️ The case against

Critics see it as building a de facto provincial police force (a long-running UCP goal) by another name, with questions about cost, duplication of the RCMP, oversight/accountability of a new Crown-corp police service, and political control over policing. Civil-liberties observers flag the importance of robust independent oversight for any expanded provincial policing.

🔐 Privacy & civil liberties: Stands up a new provincial policing entity with policing powers - surveillance, data collection and information-sharing implications depend on governance and oversight structures. Risk is medium and depends on accountability safeguards; no specific new surveillance program is created by the bill text itself.

👥 Who it affects

Alberta sheriffs/peace officers and their unions, the public interacting with provincial law enforcement, municipalities and RCMP-policed communities, and provincial-federal policing arrangements.

✔️ Fact-checkVerified: Bill 15, the Public Safety and Emergency Services Statutes Amendment Act, 2026, was introduced February 24, 2026 and received Royal Assent April 16, 2026; it enables Alberta Sheriffs to transition into the Alberta Sheriffs Police Service (ASPS), an independent Crown corporation overseen by a civilian board, creates a pathway for eligible sheriff peace officers to become police officers (subject to training/qualification), protects existing staff jobs/terms, and affirms a right to an independent association for collective bargaining. Common misinformation: the ASPS is a standalone provincial police service, not a replacement of the RCMP by legislative fiat — municipalities are not compelled by Bill 15 to switch from the RCMP, though critics (including the National Police Federation) view it as laying groundwork toward a provincial police force.
