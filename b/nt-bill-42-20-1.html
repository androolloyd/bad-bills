Bill 42-20(1)RIGHTS-POSITIVE

Tåegõhåî Got'įnê Final Self-Government Agreement Act (Tłegǫ́hłı̨ Got'įnę Final Self-Government Agreement Act)

Sponsor: Government bill (Hon. R.J. Simpson / Minister responsible for the Department of Executive and Indigenous Affairs) · Status: Passed — received Assent March 6, 2026. Territorial legislation giving legal effect to the final self-government agreement signed Sept. 2025. · Northwest Territories · Provincial

This Act gives territorial legal effect to the Tłegǫ́hłı̨ Got'įnę (TGG) Final Self-Government Agreement for the Sahtu Dene and Métis of Norman Wells, signed in September 2025 by the TGG, Canada and the GNWT after roughly two decades of negotiation. It recognizes the Tłegǫ́hłı̨ Got'įnę Government as the NWT's newest Indigenous self-government, with constitutionally protected authority to make its own laws and decisions over areas such as lands, education, justice, culture/language and internal governance. The TGG does not govern the Town of Norman Wells itself, but may assume municipal-type responsibilities if its members make up 70% or more of the town's population.

✅ The case for

Implements Indigenous peoples' inherent right to self-government and advances reconciliation and the UN Declaration on the Rights of Indigenous Peoples; ends ~20 years of negotiation, giving the community durable, Charter/Constitution-protected jurisdiction over lands, education, justice and language; strengthens Indigenous control over decisions affecting Sahtu Dene and Métis citizens; built collaboratively among three orders of government.

⚠️ The case against

Implementation and capacity-building (standing up a new government, drafting laws, funding) are significant practical challenges; jurisdictional coordination with the Town of Norman Wells and the GNWT will need to be worked out; benefits are largely confined to TGG citizens, and some non-members have raised questions about overlapping authority within the same townsite.

🔐 Privacy & civil liberties: Minimal / positive. Recognizing an Indigenous government's law-making authority can let the community set its own information-governance and data-sovereignty rules; the Act itself creates no surveillance powers.

👥 Who it affects

Tłegǫ́hłı̨ Got'įnę (Sahtu Dene and Métis) citizens in and around Norman Wells; the Town of Norman Wells; the GNWT and federal government as treaty partners.

✔️ Fact-checkVerified: the Tłegǫ́hłı̨ Got'įnę Final Self-Government Agreement was signed in September 2025 after ~20 years of negotiation, and the NWT enacted implementing legislation (Bill 42) that received Assent March 6, 2026; the TGG does not govern the Town of Norman Wells proper.
