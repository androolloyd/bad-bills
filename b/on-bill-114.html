Bill 114RIGHTS-POSITIVE

HST Relief Implementation Act (Residential Property Rebates), 2026

Sponsor: Hon. Peter Bethlenfalvy, Minister of Finance (PC) · Status: Royal Assent / law - received May 12, 2026. · Ontario · Provincial

Implements provincial HST relief/rebates tied to residential property (a housing-affordability and cost-of-living measure), reducing the sales-tax burden on qualifying new/residential housing as part of the government's affordability and homebuilding push.

✅ The case for

Provides tangible cost-of-living and housing-affordability relief; supports new-home construction and first-time/new buyers; broadly popular across parties as relief.

⚠️ The case against

Critics question whether tax rebates meaningfully improve affordability versus boosting prices or mainly benefiting developers/higher-value buyers, and note the foregone provincial revenue. Effectiveness depends on design and pass-through.

🔐 Privacy & civil liberties: Minimal privacy implications.

👥 Who it affects

Homebuyers (especially of new residential property); homebuilders/developers; the provincial treasury; the broader housing market.

✔️ Fact-checkVerified: Bill 114 received Royal Assent in May 2026 (S.O. 2026, c. 5); it amends the Retail Sales Tax Act to let the Minister, by regulation, provide credits/payments for the federal (5%) component of HST on residential property, complementing the federal new-home rebate so eligible buyers can receive up to roughly $130,000 in combined HST relief on qualifying new homes (agreements after March 31, 2026 and before April 1, 2027). Important caveat: although the enabling law has passed, the relief is not automatically payable yet - the CRA and Ontario have said further regulations and administrative steps are needed before rebates can actually be paid or credited, so buyers expecting an immediate cheque may be misinformed.
