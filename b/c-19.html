Canada Groceries and Essentials Benefit

Sponsor: Finance Minister · Status: Law — Royal Assent Feb 12, 2026 · Federal · Parliament of Canada

Creates a new income-tested benefit to help with the cost of food and essentials.

✅ The case for

Targeted cost-of-living relief.

⚠️ The case against

Debate over whether the scope and amount are adequate.

🔐 Privacy & civil liberties: Minimal.

👥 Who it affects

Lower-income households.

✔️ Fact-checkVerified: Bill C-19 (Canada Groceries and Essentials Benefit Act, amending the Income Tax Act), sponsored by Finance Minister Francois-Philippe Champagne, received Royal Assent on February 12, 2026 (Statutes of Canada 2026, c. 1). It enhances the GST/HST credit - a one-time payment of 50% of the 2025-26 credit plus a 25% annual increase for five years from July 2026 - and rebrands it as the Canada Groceries and Essentials Benefit. No notable misinformation found.
