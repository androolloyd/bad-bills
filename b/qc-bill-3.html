Bill 3RIGHTS-POSITIVE

Act to ensure effective representation of electors (electoral-map redistricting, 125 to 127 ridings)

Status: Passed — adopted unanimously and sanctioned 12 June 2026; in force for the 5 October 2026 election. · Quebec · Provincial

Increases the National Assembly from 125 to 127 seats and reworks riding boundaries (combining 115 ridings from the new electoral-commission map with 12 from the old map). It responds to a Supreme Court ruling that struck down a 2024 law for under-representing fast-growing regions (Laurentides/central Quebec), while preserving seats in the Gaspé and east-end Montreal.

✅ The case for

Brings the map into line with the constitutional right to effective representation; adopted with all-party support (CAQ, PLQ, PQ, QS), protecting both growing regions and lower-population regions/Montreal seats.

⚠️ The case against

Adds two more politicians/seats (a modest cost), and some MNAs questioned specific boundary choices; balancing regional representation against voter-parity is inherently contested.

🔐 Privacy & civil liberties: Minimal.

👥 Who it affects

All Quebec voters and the regional balance of representation; candidates and parties for the October 2026 election.

✔️ Fact-checkVerified: Bill 3 (An Act to ensure effective representation of electors) was tabled in early May 2026 by Democratic Institutions Minister Jean-Francois Roberge and adopted Friday June 12, 2026; it increases Quebec's provincial ridings from 125 to 127. It was a response to the independent electoral boundaries commission's map (which would have eliminated one Gaspe and one east-Montreal riding to create two in the Laurentides and Centre-du-Quebec) and to a Supreme Court of Canada ruling against the government's attempt to block that redraw; the bill preserves the two threatened ridings while adding two new ones. Correction: it was co-authored with the Liberals, Parti Quebecois and Quebec solidaire (a rare all-party measure), not a unilateral CAQ gerrymander.
