Bill 1 (Winter 2026)LOWER CONCERN

Appropriation (Interim Operations and Maintenance) Act, 2026-2027 (interim O&M budget) - with companion winter supplementary bills

Sponsor: Premier John Main, Minister of Finance · Status: PASSED - Approved and given royal assent during the winter sitting (Feb 22 - Mar 16, 2026); Commissioner Eva Aariak gave assent in mid-March 2026. · Nunavut · Provincial

Because the October 2025 election delayed the normal budget cycle, the GN's winter sitting passed a $724.9-million INTERIM operations and maintenance budget to keep all 14 departments running for 2026-27 until the full budget could be tabled in spring. The same sitting also passed a $373.2M capital plan (across 11 departments, largest share to Community Services for water/wastewater and fire halls) and two supplementary bills for the 2025-26 year ($158.6M supplementary O&M across 5 departments; $41.9M supplementary capital across 7 departments). These were superseded/augmented by the full spring budget.

✅ The case for

Prevented any funding gap or government shutdown during the post-election transition; ensured continuous pay for public servants and uninterrupted essential services; the capital portion funded clean-water infrastructure, a top public-health priority.

⚠️ The case against

An interim 'stopgap' provides limited detail and limited time for scrutiny; the need for it reflected the election-driven delay in the full budget; supplementary appropriations highlight in-year overspending/underbudgeting in several departments.

🔐 Privacy & civil liberties: Minimal. Appropriation acts authorize spending only and create no data-collection or civil-liberties powers.

👥 Who it affects

All Nunavummiut and GN departments/employees during the Apr-spring 2026 transition period; communities receiving water and capital infrastructure.
