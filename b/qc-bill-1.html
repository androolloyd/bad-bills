Bill 1HIGH CONCERN

Constitutional Act of 2025 respecting Quebec (a written Quebec constitution)

Status: DIED on the order paper — abandoned 11 June 2026 by Justice Minister Simon Jolin-Barrette; will not be adopted this Parliament. · Quebec · Provincial

Would have codified a written Quebec constitution by ordinary legislation, asserting provincial autonomy: directives letting the Quebec government instruct state bodies to refuse federal cooperation on certain matters, and a proposed Quebec Constitutional Council to assess perceived federal overreach. An early abortion-rights provision was added then removed.

✅ The case for

Supporters argued it would entrench Quebec's distinct identity and strengthen resistance to federal centralization (citing B.C.'s 1996 constitution as precedent for using ordinary legislation).

⚠️ The case against

The Barreau du Québec warned provisions could threaten the separation of powers, muzzle checks and balances, create legal instability and weaken fundamental rights. Hundreds of civil-society groups (including the FTQ labour federation) demanded full withdrawal; Indigenous nations criticized their 'phantom' treatment in the text. The minister's framing of public participation as 'obstruction' drew further criticism. Attempts to water it down failed and it collapsed.

🔐 Privacy & civil liberties: Privacy: minimal. The civil-liberties concerns were structural — separation of powers, judicial oversight, legal certainty, fundamental-rights protection and Indigenous rights.

👥 Who it affects

All Quebecers (constitutional architecture and rights), the judiciary, Indigenous nations, civil-society and labour groups, and federal-provincial relations.

✔️ Fact-checkVerified: Bill 1 (Quebec Constitution Act, 2025), a written provincial constitution declaring Quebec an autonomous national state and asserting primacy over inconsistent rules of law (entrenching French, state secularism, gender equality and abortion access), was tabled October 2025 under then-Premier Francois Legault. IMPORTANT correction to common misinformation: although the bill was adopted in principle on April 1, 2026 (vote 68-31), it was NEVER passed into law - Justice Minister Simon Jolin-Barrette abandoned it and it died on the order paper on June 11, 2026 when Premier Christine Frechette declined to use closure to force it through before the session ended. So it is incorrect to say Quebec 'adopted' or 'enacted' a constitution; the bill failed.
