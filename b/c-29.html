C-29WATCH CLOSELY

Financial Crimes Agency Act

Sponsor: Government bill · Status: Second reading · Federal · Parliament of Canada

Creates a new standalone federal financial-crime police agency (money laundering, fraud, sanctions evasion, crypto laundering, cyber-enabled crime) with investigative, policing and asset-recovery powers, wired into the Privacy Act, Criminal Code, immigration and sanctions laws.

✅ The case for

Canada has been criticized as weak on money laundering ("snow-washing"); a dedicated agency could finally deliver enforcement.

⚠️ The case against

A powerful new body plugged into financial and personal data — oversight and accountability are the open questions.

🔐 Privacy & civil liberties: Broad data access across financial and personal records.

👥 Who it affects

The financial sector, suspects, and ordinary account-holders indirectly.

✔️ Fact-checkVerified: Bill C-29 (Financial Crimes Agency Act), sponsored by Finance Minister Francois-Philippe Champagne, was introduced and given first reading on April 27, 2026 and is at second reading in the House - an early stage, not yet law. It would create a new standalone federal law-enforcement body to investigate complex financial crimes (money laundering, sanctions evasion, fraud), described as the first new federal police agency since the RCMP.
