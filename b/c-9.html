C-9WATCH CLOSELY

Combatting Hate Act

Sponsor: Justice Minister Sean Fraser · Status: Passed House & Senate (amended June 4) — awaiting final concurrence + Royal Assent · Federal · Parliament of Canada

Amends the Criminal Code on hate propaganda and hate crime, creates offences for obstructing access to places of worship, schools and community centres, and toughens penalties for hate-motivated crime.

✅ The case for

Responds to a documented rise in hate incidents and protects people's ability to worship and gather safely; welcomed by many faith and community groups.

⚠️ The case against

The CCLA, Canadian Constitution Foundation, Canadian Labour Congress and several religious groups warn the wording is broad and could chill free expression, good-faith religious teaching, and peaceful protest or picket lines.

🔐 Privacy & civil liberties: The fight here is free expression and freedom of assembly, not data privacy — outcome depends heavily on how police and courts apply it.

👥 Who it affects

Minority and faith communities (intended protection); protesters, clergy and online commentators (expression risk).

✔️ Fact-checkVerified: Bill C-9 (Combatting Hate Act), sponsored by Justice Minister Sean Fraser, amends the Criminal Code on hate propaganda, hate crime and access to religious/cultural places. As of June 16, 2026 it is at the stage of the House considering Senate amendments (the Senate passed it with a hate-symbols amendment); it has NOT yet received Royal Assent, so reports that it is 'already law' are premature. Its removal of the good-faith religious-expression defence is the most contested element.
