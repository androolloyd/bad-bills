Bill 130WATCH CLOSELY

Traffic Safety Act

Sponsor: PC government · Status: Law — Royal Assent Oct 3, 2025 (takes effect 2026) · Nova Scotia · Provincial

Replaces the 1930s-era Motor Vehicle Act and, for the first time in Nova Scotia, authorizes photo radar, red-light cameras and intersection cameras.

✅ The case for

Overdue modernization; automated enforcement can cut speeding and collisions at dangerous spots.

⚠️ The case against

Automated cameras mean routine surveillance of drivers — concerns about data retention, who sees footage, function creep and revenue-driven enforcement.

🔐 Privacy & civil liberties: New automated camera surveillance of drivers — the privacy details live in the 2026 regulations to come.

👥 Who it affects

Every driver in Nova Scotia.

✔️ Fact-checkVerified: Bill 130, the Traffic Safety Act, received Royal Assent Oct. 3, 2025 (2025 c.20) and will replace the century-old Motor Vehicle Act when proclaimed (the government targeted 2026); for the first time it authorizes automated enforcement (photo radar, red-light and intersection cameras) and lets municipalities set lower speed limits and regulate bikes/scooters. Correcting a common misconception: passage does not by itself put cameras on the road — the enabling provisions require regulations/proclamation before any automated enforcement begins; it is a fresh Act after the 2018 Traffic Safety Act proved too difficult to proclaim.
