Bill 11LOWER CONCERN

Administrative Efficiency and Accountability in Healthcare Act

Sponsor: PC government · Status: Law · Nova Scotia · Provincial

Restructures health-system administration as part of the government's health-care overhaul.

✅ The case for

Aims to cut red tape and improve accountability in a strained system.

⚠️ The case against

Critics question centralization and whether it improves front-line care.

🔐 Privacy & civil liberties: Watch health-data handling as systems are consolidated.

👥 Who it affects

Patients and health-care workers.

✔️ Fact-checkVerified: Bill 11, the Administrative Efficiency and Accountability in Healthcare Act, received Royal Assent March 26, 2025 (2025 c.2); it repeals outdated statutes (including the 19th-century Anatomy Act and the Emergency Department Accountability Act) and amends the Health Services and Insurance Act and Human Organ and Tissue Donation Act to cut administrative burden. Correcting the spin in the title: opposition critics note that despite 'Accountability' in the name, the bill eliminates the legally required annual emergency-department accountability report — the government argues equivalent data is now available in real time via online dashboards.
