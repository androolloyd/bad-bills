C-4RIGHTS-POSITIVE

Making Life More Affordable for Canadians Act

Sponsor: Finance Minister · Status: Law — Royal Assent March 12, 2026 · Federal · Parliament of Canada

Cuts the lowest income-tax rate from 15% to 14%, eliminates GST for first-time buyers on new homes up to $1M (reduced up to $1.5M), repeals the consumer carbon price, and changes political parties' personal-information rules.

✅ The case for

Direct cost-of-living relief; the carbon-price repeal was widely popular.

⚠️ The case against

Repeal removes a climate tool and the rebate many lower-income households received; the home-buyer break may partly inflate prices.

🔐 Privacy & civil liberties: Nationalizes (modest) privacy rules for political parties.

👥 Who it affects

Taxpayers, first-time home buyers, and lower-income households.

✔️ Fact-checkVerified: Bill C-4 (Making Life More Affordable for Canadians Act), sponsored by Finance Minister Francois-Philippe Champagne, received Royal Assent on March 12, 2026 (Statutes of Canada 2026, c. 2). It cuts the lowest federal personal income-tax rate, provides GST relief for first-time buyers on new homes up to $1.5 million, and removes the consumer carbon price from federal law.
