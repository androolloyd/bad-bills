Bill 18HIGH CONCERN

Safeguards for Last Resort Termination of Life Act (MAID)

Sponsor: Justice Minister / Government (UCP) · Status: Passed third reading April 22, 2026; received Royal Assent in the spring sitting. Comes into force on proclamation. (Official site confirms the bill; third-reading passage widely reported.) · Alberta · Provincial

Adds provincial 'safeguards' to medical assistance in dying. It restricts/eliminates so-called 'Track 2' MAID (people whose natural death is NOT reasonably foreseeable), and would prohibit MAID for minors (including mature minors), people whose sole underlying condition is mental illness, advance requests, and adults lacking decision-making capacity. Some reporting describes limiting access to patients with a foreseeable death within ~12 months.

✅ The case for

Supporters (including some faith-based and disability-rights groups, e.g. CCCC) argue it adds protective guardrails against premature or coerced deaths, especially for people with disabilities, mental illness, or those who may feel pressured by poverty or inadequate care; reasserts caution where federal expansion has been contested.

⚠️ The case against

Critics argue Alberta is overriding/curtailing a right the Supreme Court (Carter) recognized and that federal law permits, potentially forcing suffering patients to endure prolonged decline or seek care elsewhere; raises division-of-powers and Charter questions (s.7 life/liberty/security). Restricting Track 2 narrows access already available federally.

🔐 Privacy & civil liberties: Largely about eligibility, not surveillance. Civil-liberties concern is bodily autonomy and the s.7 right to choose the timing/manner of one's death rather than data privacy. Possible compelled disclosure of diagnosis/capacity to assessors, but no new mass-data regime. Privacy proper: Minimal; autonomy: significant.

👥 Who it affects

Terminally and chronically ill Albertans seeking MAID (especially Track 2 / non-foreseeable-death patients), people with disabilities and mental illness, mature minors, families, and physicians/nurse practitioners who assess and provide MAID.

✔️ Fact-checkVerified: Bill 18, the Safeguards for Last Resort Termination of Life Act, was introduced March 18, 2026 and passed third reading April 22, 2026; it limits MAID to 'Track 1' cases where natural death is reasonably foreseeable (interpreted as likely within 12 months), prohibits 'Track 2' MAID, and bars MAID for mature minors, mental-illness-as-sole-condition, and advance requests, while designating MAID-free facilities. Common misinformation: the bill does not 'ban MAID' in Alberta outright, and because MAID eligibility is set by the federal Criminal Code, legal commentators note the province's authority to restrict it is contested and the law invites a Charter challenge under Carter/Truchon rather than being settled law.
