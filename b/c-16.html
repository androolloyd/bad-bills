C-16RIGHTS-POSITIVE

Protecting Victims Act

Sponsor: Justice Minister · Status: In committee · Federal · Parliament of Canada
🗳️ House of Commons vote (3rd reading): Passed 192–133 · 12 paired · 2026-06-11 · official record

Improves disclosure to victims and the submission of victim statements in the corrections/parole process, with child-protection and gender-based-violence measures.

✅ The case for

Strengthens victims' rights in the justice system.

⚠️ The case against

Limited.

🔐 Privacy & civil liberties: Handling of victim information.

👥 Who it affects

Crime victims.

✔️ Fact-checkVerified: Bill C-16 (Protecting Victims Act), sponsored by Justice Minister Sean Fraser, has passed the House and is at committee study in the Senate (referred June 15, 2026) - not yet law. It amends the Criminal Code on child protection (restoring some mandatory minimums with a constitutional 'safety valve,' expanding luring/sextortion offences), creates a coercive-control offence for intimate partners, and strengthens the Canadian Victims Bill of Rights.
