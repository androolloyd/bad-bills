Bill 10RIGHTS-POSITIVE

Act protecting consumers against abusive ticket-resale and online-subscription practices

Status: Passed — sanctioned; key provisions take effect 12 September 2026 (bill was re-inscribed to the 3rd Session on 6 May 2026 and completed). · Quebec · Provincial

Sponsored by Justice Minister Simon Jolin-Barrette, it cracks down on abusive ticket resale (transparency about resale platforms and ticket details; bans fees for transferring a show ticket) and on 'subscription traps' (an easy online cancellation/'cancel button' and restitution obligations), enforced through the Consumer Protection Act with administrative monetary penalties.

✅ The case for

Concrete consumer relief: curbs scalping/hidden resale, stops fees on ticket transfers, and makes it easy to cancel online subscriptions; broadly welcomed (with refinements suggested by the music industry/ADISQ).

⚠️ The case against

Mostly technical debate over scope and enforcement; limited controversy.

🔐 Privacy & civil liberties: Minimal — generally rights-positive for consumers; the main consideration is fair handling of consumer data by platforms and the regulator.

👥 Who it affects

Consumers buying event tickets and online subscriptions, resale platforms, ticketing companies, and subscription businesses.

✔️ Fact-checkVerified: Bill 10 (An Act to protect consumers against abusive practices in ticket reselling and online subscription renewal) was tabled December 2, 2025 and was passed in the June 2026 session ('it ultimately managed to cross the finish line'). It amends the Consumer Protection Act to require an easy, clearly labelled cancellation button for subscriptions, advance written notice (about 2-10 days) before a free trial or promo price ends, restrictions on reselling tickets above face value without the producer's authorization, protection of consumers' right to post reviews, and stronger restitution remedies. No notable misinformation found; note some provisions are expected to be phased in and the in-force dates set by regulation.
