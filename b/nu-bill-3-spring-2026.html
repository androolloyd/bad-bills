Bill 3 (Spring 2026)LOWER CONCERN

Miscellaneous Statutes Amendment Act

Sponsor: Government bill (Department of Justice; Justice Minister, 7th Assembly cabinet) · Status: PASSED - Royal assent June 11, 2026. · Nunavut · Provincial

An omnibus 'housekeeping' bill that makes minor, technical and plain-language corrections across multiple existing territorial statutes - fixing inconsistencies, updating outdated references and modernizing wording. It is not a policy bill; such acts are routinely used in Westminster-style legislatures to tidy the statute book without substantive policy change.

✅ The case for

Improves clarity, accuracy and consistency of Nunavut's laws; reduces legal ambiguity; an efficient way to make many small fixes in one bill rather than separate legislation.

⚠️ The case against

Omnibus statute-amendment bills can bundle many unrelated changes, making line-by-line public scrutiny harder; by design it makes no substantive policy improvements. Specific clause-by-clause details were not widely reported.

🔐 Privacy & civil liberties: Minimal. Reported changes are technical/plain-language updates; no new data-collection, surveillance or rights-affecting powers identified. (If any amended statute touches information handling it would be incidental and technical.)

👥 Who it affects

Primarily lawyers, courts, drafters and regulated entities relying on precise statutory language; indirect effect on the general public through clearer law.

Detailed clause list not publicly itemized in coverage; the official bill text is the authoritative source.
