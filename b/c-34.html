C-34HIGH CONCERN

Safe Social Media Act (enacts the Digital Safety Act)

Sponsor: Minister of Canadian Heritage · Status: In progress (introduced ~June 2026) · Federal · Parliament of Canada

Would ban under-16s from social media; require platforms to use age verification/estimation (ID upload or AI face-scan); create a Digital Safety Commission (fines up to 3% of global revenue); and add AI-chatbot rules. Verification data is supposed to be discarded after the check.

✅ The case for

Targets youth mental health and online harms, with a regulator that has real teeth.

⚠️ The case against

The CCLA and Michael Geist warn that keeping kids out means every adult must verify too — effectively mandating ID or face-scans to use the internet — and the verification infrastructure is permanent even if the ban is "temporary."

🔐 Privacy & civil liberties: Age verification becomes identity verification for everyone; biometric/face-scan risks; chills anonymous speech (whistleblowers, abuse survivors, dissidents).

👥 Who it affects

Every social-media user, teens under 16, and platforms.

✔️ Fact-checkVerified: Bill C-34 (Safe Social Media Act), introduced June 10, 2026, is only at second reading in the House of Commons; it is NOT yet law. It would enact the Digital Safety Act and Digital Safety Commission of Canada Act, including a proposed under-16 social-media restriction, mandatory age verification, AI-chatbot rules and platform duties - so reports that an under-16 ban is already 'here' or in force are inaccurate.
