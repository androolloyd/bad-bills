C-12HIGH CONCERN

Strengthening Canada's Immigration System and Borders Act

Sponsor: Public Safety Minister Gary Anandasangaree · Status: Law — Royal Assent March 26, 2026 · Federal · Parliament of Canada

The border/immigration half of C-2, now law. Sets new asylum time limits — people in Canada more than a year are barred from a refugee hearing — lets the government pause, cancel or change immigration applications and documents "in the public interest," expands data-sharing, and gives Canada Post authority to open mail in some cases.

✅ The case for

Faster removals, asylum-system integrity, and alignment with U.S. border demands.

⚠️ The case against

The one-year bar can trap genuine refugees; broad ministerial discretion; the Canadian Council for Refugees opposed it.

🔐 Privacy & civil liberties: Cross-agency/government data-sharing, mail-opening powers, and reduced asylum due process.

👥 Who it affects

Immigrants, refugees, and anyone who receives mail.

✔️ Fact-checkVerified: Bill C-12 (Strengthening Canada's Immigration System and Borders Act) received Royal Assent on March 26, 2026 (Statutes of Canada 2026, c. 4) and is now law. It carries forward the border/immigration measures of Bill C-2 but NOT the lawful-access surveillance parts; among other changes it makes asylum claims filed more than one year after first arrival ineligible for referral to the Immigration and Refugee Board.
