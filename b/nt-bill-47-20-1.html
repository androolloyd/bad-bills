Bill 47-20(1)LOWER CONCERN

Appropriation Act (Operations Expenditures), 2026-2027

Sponsor: Hon. Caroline Wawzonek, Minister of Finance & Deputy Premier (Government bill) · Status: Passed — received Assent March 6, 2026 (all three readings completed March 5, 2026; passed 15-3). Now in force. · Northwest Territories · Provincial

The annual operating budget for the Government of the Northwest Territories. Budget 2026-27 was tabled Feb. 5, 2026 and authorizes roughly $2.39 billion in operations spending (about $2.72 billion total when infrastructure, deferred maintenance and the supplementary reserve are included). It introduces no new taxes; some fees and property mill rates are indexed to inflation. During committee review regular MLAs negotiated about $40 million in additional spending, including roughly $30 million to implement the territory's inclusive-schooling review (specialist services, speech-language pathology, early literacy screening), primary-care and nurse-practitioner staffing, support for Aurora College's polytechnic transition, transfers of community learning centres to Indigenous/local governments, and RCMP overnight accommodation in Gamètì and Tsiigehtchic.

✅ The case for

Holds the line on taxes amid a high cost of living; keeps total territorial debt (projected ~$2.21B by March 31, 2027) within the $3.1B federal borrowing limit; directs new money to health staffing and inclusive schooling; reflects genuine consensus-government give-and-take, with regular MLAs extracting $40M in changes; advances devolution by transferring some programs to Indigenous and community governments.

⚠️ The case against

Three regular MLAs (Robert Hawkins, Kieron Testart, Richard Edjericon) voted against it, arguing it did not go far enough — Hawkins said the government 'got away very cheap,' with fewer than a quarter of regular MLAs' requests met; Edjericon cited a lack of investment in his (Tu Nedhé-Wiilideh) communities. Critics note continued reliance on federal transfers and limited room for new program spending given debt pressures.

🔐 Privacy & civil liberties: Minimal. As an appropriation statute it authorizes spending and does not create surveillance powers or collect personal information.

👥 Who it affects

All NWT residents (~44,000), GNWT departments and employees, students, health-care patients, Indigenous and community governments receiving program transfers.

✔️ Fact-checkVerified: the 2026-27 operating budget (Bill 47) passed third reading March 5, 2026 by a 15-3 vote with ~$40M added during debate and received Assent March 6, 2026; it contains no new taxes.
