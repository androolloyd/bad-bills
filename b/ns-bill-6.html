Bill 6WATCH CLOSELY

Agriculture, Energy and Natural Resources Act

Sponsor: PC government · Status: Law — effective March 26, 2025 · Nova Scotia · Provincial

Lifts the 40-plus-year ban on uranium exploration/mining and the 2014 ban on onshore fracking, pitched as resource self-reliance amid U.S. tariffs.

✅ The case for

Potential resource revenue, jobs and energy security; allows research into uranium deposits.

⚠️ The case against

99 public submissions against, 1 in favour; strong opposition from Mi'kmaq communities, environmental groups and rural residents over water, health and Indigenous rights.

🔐 Privacy & civil liberties: Minimal data-privacy angle; the civil-liberties concern is Indigenous consent (duty to consult) and environmental protection.

👥 Who it affects

Rural and Mi'kmaq communities, the environment, the resource sector.

✔️ Fact-checkVerified: Bill 6, An Act Respecting Agriculture, Energy and Natural Resources, received Royal Assent March 26, 2025 (2025 c.4); it repealed the Uranium Exploration and Mining Prohibition Act and amended the Petroleum Resources Act to enable (with authorization) onshore drilling and hydraulic fracturing, effectively ending the long-standing uranium ban and the 2014 fracking moratorium, and also created agrologist regulation and a noxious-weed framework. Correcting misinformation: the fracking provisions concern ONSHORE petroleum (not offshore), and the bill does not 'ban' fracking — it requires ministerial/regulatory authorization for it; Mi'kmaw chiefs say they were not consulted.
