Bill 4 (Spring 2026)RIGHTS-POSITIVE

An Act to Amend the Income Tax Act (Nunavut Child Benefit)

Sponsor: Premier John Main, Minister of Finance · Status: PASSED - Royal assent June 11, 2026. Takes effect July 1, 2026; payments begin accruing in July with the first increased payments reaching families in September 2026 (retroactive to July). · Nunavut · Provincial

Amends Nunavut's Income Tax Act to roughly double the basic Nunavut Child Benefit (NUCB) from $348 to $696 per child per year - addressing the fact that Nunavut's child benefit was the lowest in Canada. It also removes a 3% consumer-price-index 'floor' so the benefit now indexes automatically to inflation/CPI each year. The benefit is paid to families with net income of $22,065 or less for each child under 18, is fully GN-funded, and is administered by the Canada Revenue Agency. The GN estimates ~$2.3M in additional annual cost.

✅ The case for

Directly increases income for the lowest-income families in a territory with the highest cost of living and severe food insecurity; doubling plus automatic inflation indexing protects the benefit's real value over time; uses existing CRA administration so delivery is low-cost; Premier explicitly tied it to child poverty and food insecurity. A clear rights/benefit-expanding (anti-poverty) measure.

⚠️ The case against

Even doubled, ~$696/child/year is modest against Nunavut's extreme costs - critics and commenters called it insufficient; the $22,065 net-income eligibility ceiling is low and excludes many struggling families, prompting calls to widen eligibility; the increase lags years of high northern inflation; Premier Main himself acknowledged the changes are 'small.'

🔐 Privacy & civil liberties: Minimal/positive. Eligibility relies on income data already collected by the CRA for existing benefit administration; the amendment creates no new surveillance or data-sharing powers. As a means-tested benefit it involves routine income verification only.

👥 Who it affects

Lower-income families with children under 18 in Nunavut (net income at or below $22,065); children living in poverty; administered via the CRA.

✔️ Fact-checkVerified: the basic Nunavut Child Benefit rises from $348 to $696 per child annually and the 3% CPI floor is removed to index it to inflation; assent June 11, 2026, effective July 1, 2026, first increased payments September 2026. Confirmed by Nunatsiaq News, CBC and the GN.
