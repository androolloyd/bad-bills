Bill 1 (Spring 2026)LOWER CONCERN

Appropriation (Operations and Maintenance) Act, 2026-2027

Sponsor: Premier John Main, Minister of Finance · Status: PASSED - Royal assent granted June 11, 2026 by Deputy Commissioner Lew Phillip on the final day of the spring sitting. · Nunavut · Provincial

Nunavut's full operations and maintenance budget for the 2026-27 fiscal year: roughly $2.47 billion in operating spending, an 8% increase over the prior year. Tabled by Premier/Finance Minister John Main on May 25, 2026 as his first full budget. Health receives the largest share (~$663M, up ~$83M/14%), followed by Education (~$310M, +$20M, including teacher-salary money and three new schools), Nunavut Housing Corporation (~$296M operating), Transportation & Infrastructure (~$281M), and Family Services (~$252M). Main framed it as a 'disciplined' budget of fiscal restraint, symbolically wearing worn shoes ('not the time for new shoes'). It replaces the $724.9M interim O&M budget passed in the March winter sitting. Revenue (~$3.5B) is dominated by federal transfers.

✅ The case for

Funds essential public services across 14 departments in a territory with extreme costs and acute needs; largest-ever health allocation targets the suicide/mental-health crisis, physician services, long-term care, and phased opening of the Aqqusariaq addictions and trauma treatment facility; new school and teacher funding; framed as fiscally disciplined while still growing 8%.

⚠️ The case against

Nunavut remains almost entirely dependent on federal transfers (own-source revenue is small), limiting fiscal autonomy; chronic capacity gaps mean appropriated funds are not always fully spent or delivered (housing, health staffing); critics note spending growth still struggles to keep pace with population growth, inflation, and a deep housing shortage; consensus-government budgets face limited adversarial scrutiny (no opposition party).

🔐 Privacy & civil liberties: Minimal. An appropriation act authorizes government spending and creates no new data-collection, surveillance, or civil-liberties powers.

👥 Who it affects

All Nunavummiut - funds health, education, housing, family services, transportation, justice and all other GN departments; GN employees; communities reliant on public services.
