Bill 249RIGHTS-POSITIVE

Distribution of Fake Intimate Images Act

Sponsor: Suzy Hansen (NDP) — private member's bill · Status: Private member's bill (before the House) · Nova Scotia · Provincial

Would amend the Intimate Images and Cyber-protection Act to cover AI-generated and deepfake intimate images.

✅ The case for

Closes a real gap as deepfake abuse rises; protects victims.

⚠️ The case against

As a private member's bill it is less likely to advance without government support.

🔐 Privacy & civil liberties: Rights-positive — strengthens protection against non-consensual and AI-faked intimate images.

👥 Who it affects

Victims of image-based and deepfake abuse.

✔️ Fact-checkVerified: Bill 249 is a Private Member's Bill from Suzy Hansen (NDP, Halifax Needham) that would amend the Intimate Images and Cyber-protection Act to cover 'artificially generated' (AI/deepfake) intimate images; it had First Reading March 26, 2026 and, as a private member's bill, has not advanced further (it is not law). It defines a 'fake intimate image' as a reasonably convincing AI/software-created depiction and would let courts act even when the person is not identifiable. Correcting a likely confusion: this is a provincial civil/cyber-protection measure and is distinct from the federal Criminal Code deepfake bill (Bill C-16); both were spurred by a 2026 Nova Scotia case where a man was acquitted because the existing law did not cover AI-generated images.
