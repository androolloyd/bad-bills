Bill 40RIGHTS-POSITIVE

The Elections Amendment Act (candidate ethics disclosure)

Sponsor: Hon. Matt Wiebe, Minister of Justice (NDP) · Status: Royal Assent June 1, 2026 — now law (applies to subsequent elections, with no 90-day delay). · Manitoba · Provincial

Adds an election-disclosure rule for candidates who are or were MLAs: they must disclose if the Ethics Commissioner found they contravened the conflict-of-interest law in a case where the Assembly imposed a fine, suspended their voting rights, or declared their seat vacant. A mere reprimand does not trigger disclosure.

✅ The case for

Improves democratic accountability and voter information by surfacing serious, formally-sanctioned conflict-of-interest findings before people vote. Proportionate — excludes minor matters resolved by reprimand.

⚠️ The case against

Narrow in scope (only former/sitting MLAs, only the most serious sanctioned findings); critics could argue it doesn't capture broader ethics issues or non-MLA candidates. Low controversy overall.

🔐 Privacy & civil liberties: Minimal/positive: discloses official, already-adjudicated public ethics findings, not private personal data.

👥 Who it affects

Candidates who are or were MLAs; voters seeking information about candidates' ethics records.

✔️ Fact-checkVerified: Bill 40, The Elections Amendment Act (Justice Minister Wiebe), received royal assent June 1, 2026 (SM 2026, c. 33) and applies to subsequent elections. It requires prospective candidates who are or were MLAs to disclose whether the Ethics Commissioner found they contravened The Conflict of Interest (Members and Ministers) Act in cases where the Assembly ordered a fine, a suspension, or that the member's seat be vacated. Clarification: disclosure is NOT required where the only sanction was a reprimand, so it captures only the more serious ethics findings.
