C-3RIGHTS-POSITIVE

Citizenship Act amendment ('Lost Canadians')

Sponsor: Immigration Minister · Status: Law — Royal Assent Nov 20, 2025 (S.C. 2025, c.5) · Federal · Parliament of Canada

Restores citizenship-by-descent rights that courts had struck down, fixing the "Lost Canadians" problem.

✅ The case for

Corrects a rights violation and helps affected families.

⚠️ The case against

Limited opposition.

🔐 Privacy & civil liberties: Minimal.

👥 Who it affects

Canadians born abroad and their children.

✔️ Fact-checkVerified: Bill C-3 (An Act to amend the Citizenship Act, 2025), sponsored by Immigration Minister Lena Metlege Diab, received Royal Assent on November 20, 2025 (Statutes of Canada 2025, c. 5); IRCC began applying the amended Act from December 15, 2025. It ends the first-generation limit on citizenship by descent - restoring status to many 'Lost Canadians' - and lets citizens born abroad pass on citizenship if they show a 'substantial connection' (1,095 days physical presence). The royal-assent date is sometimes reported as Nov. 21; the official record shows Nov. 20, 2025.
