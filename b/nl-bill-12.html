Bill 12RIGHTS-POSITIVE

Children's Law Act and Family Law Act (Amendment) — modernized family law

Sponsor: Hon. Helen Conway Ottenheimer, Minister of Justice and Public Safety & Attorney General (PC) · Status: Royal Assent (June 2, 2026) · Newfoundland and Labrador · Provincial

Aligns provincial family law with the federal Divorce Act. Replaces 'custody/access' with 'decision-making responsibility,' 'parenting time' and 'contact'; centres all orders on the best interests of the child; adds a broad family-violence framework (physical, sexual, psychological, financial abuse and exposure to it); creates 60-day relocation-notice rules; and pushes family dispute resolution (mediation, collaborative law).

✅ The case for

Child-centred, trauma-informed modernization; explicit family-violence and Indigenous-heritage considerations; clearer relocation rules and emphasis on out-of-court resolution can reduce conflict and cost. Broadly supported as overdue harmonization.

⚠️ The case against

Broad family-violence definitions and mandatory information duties increase the evidentiary and procedural load on self-represented parents; effectiveness depends on access to affordable mediation and legal aid. Some worry expansive definitions could be weaponized in high-conflict cases.

🔐 Privacy & civil liberties: Low/medium. Parties must provide 'complete, accurate and up-to-date' information, and courts must review records on restraining orders, child protection and criminal matters — creating cross-database information flows about families, though within judicial proceedings and existing court-confidentiality norms.

👥 Who it affects

Separating/divorcing parents, grandparents and other caregivers, children, family-law lawyers and the courts.

✔️ Fact-checkVerified: Bill 12 (An Act to Amend the Children's Law Act and the Family Law Act) passed third reading May 7, 2026 and received Royal Assent June 2, 2026 (SNL c.10). It replaces 'custody/access' with 'decision-making responsibility', 'parenting time' and 'contact', adds a statutory definition of family violence that courts must weigh in best-interests determinations, creates child-relocation notice/objection procedures, and adds duties to encourage family dispute resolution — broadly aligning provincial law with the modernized federal Divorce Act. No notable misinformation found (this NL Bill 12 is unrelated to a Quebec 'Bill 12' on filiation that appears in searches).
