Bill 611RIGHTS-POSITIVE

The Constitutional Questions (Notwithstanding Clause Referral) Amendment Act (private member's bill)

Sponsor: Nicole Sarauer (NDP House Leader), Saskatchewan NDP · Status: First reading December 4, 2025; did NOT advance - the Sask Party government rejected/declined to advance it, so it died on the order paper. · Saskatchewan · Provincial

Opposition bill that would require any future Saskatchewan law invoking the Charter's notwithstanding clause (s.33) to be automatically referred to the Saskatchewan Court of Appeal within 90 days, asking the court whether the law violates Charter s.2 or ss.7-15 and, if so, whether it is 'reasonable and demonstrably justified' under s.1. It would not block use of the clause, but would force a public judicial opinion on the rights impact.

✅ The case for

A pro-accountability, rights-protective measure: it would create transparency and an independent judicial check whenever a government overrides Charter rights, without removing the legislature's power. Framed against Alberta's use of the notwithstanding clause to override teachers' rights, and supported by labour (CUPE Saskatchewan).

⚠️ The case against

Government argues the notwithstanding clause is a legitimate tool and that mandatory referrals would add cost, delay and judicial entanglement to validly enacted laws. Opponents of the clause itself argue a non-binding court opinion is a weak safeguard. As an opposition bill, it had no path to passage.

🔐 Privacy & civil liberties: Positive for civil liberties in design: it is specifically aimed at protecting Charter rights (fundamental freedoms, legal rights, equality) by ensuring judicial scrutiny whenever a government pre-emptively overrides them. No privacy downside; it is a democratic-accountability and rights-protection mechanism.

👥 Who it affects

All Saskatchewan residents whose Charter rights could be affected by future use of the notwithstanding clause; workers/unions; the courts and the legislature.

✔️ Fact-checkVerified: Bill 611, The Constitutional Questions (Notwithstanding Clause Referral) Amendment Act, is an NDP private member's bill (sponsored by House leader Nicole Sarauer) introduced in early December 2025. It would require that whenever Saskatchewan passes a law invoking section 33 of the Charter (the notwithstanding clause), the government must refer that law to the Court of Appeal within 90 days for an opinion on whether it violates Charter sections 2 or 7-15 and is justified under section 1. IMPORTANT misinformation correction: contrary to some circulating claims that it 'received assent,' the Sask. Party majority government rejected the bill (Justice Minister Tim McLeod opposed it), and as a defeated/stalled Opposition private member's bill it did NOT become law.
