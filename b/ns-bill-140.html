Private Data Protection Act

Sponsor: Derek Mombourquette (Liberal) — private member's bill · Status: Private member's bill (before the House) · Nova Scotia · Provincial

Would create personal-data protection rights in the private sector provincially.

✅ The case for

Fills gaps in provincial private-sector privacy coverage.

⚠️ The case against

Unlikely to advance without government backing.

🔐 Privacy & civil liberties: Rights-positive private-sector privacy proposal.

👥 Who it affects

Anyone whose data is held by NS businesses.

✔️ Fact-checkVerified: Bill 140, the Private Data Protection Act, is a Private Member's Bill from Derek Mombourquette (Liberal, Sydney-Membertou); it had First Reading Sept. 24, 2025 and has not advanced beyond that, so it is not law. It would create a statutory tort — actionable without proof of damage — for willfully violating another person's privacy (e.g., surveillance or data misuse), and was tabled as part of a Liberal package responding to the Nova Scotia Power data breach. No notable misinformation found, other than the common error of assuming a private member's bill at first reading is in force.
