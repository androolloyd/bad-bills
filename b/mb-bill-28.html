Bill 28RIGHTS-POSITIVE

The Health System Governance and Accountability Amendment Act (Nurse-to-Patient Ratios)

Sponsor: Hon. Uzoma Asagwara, Deputy Premier & Minister of Health, Seniors and Long-Term Care (NDP) · Status: Royal Assent June 1, 2026; marked 'P' — passed, awaiting proclamation. (Companion Bill 26 ending mandatory nurse overtime, and Bill 27 a patient bill of rights, were also passed.) · Manitoba · Provincial

Enables enforceable minimum nurse-to-patient staffing ratios in the health system, part of an NDP package that also ends mandatory overtime for nurses (Bill 26) and declares principles for patient care (Bill 27). Aims to improve safety and retention by capping how many patients a nurse must cover.

✅ The case for

Backed by nurses' unions and patient-safety advocates; addresses burnout, mandatory overtime and understaffing widely blamed for ER and care problems. Could improve care quality and help retain front-line staff.

⚠️ The case against

Effectiveness hinges on regulations setting the actual ratios and on whether enough nurses exist to meet them amid shortages — critics question feasibility and cost, and warn ratios without recruitment could force bed closures or simply shift strain. Marked 'P,' so timing/teeth depend on proclamation and regulations.

🔐 Privacy & civil liberties: Minimal privacy/civil-liberties implications (health-system staffing measure).

👥 Who it affects

Nurses and front-line health workers; hospital and long-term-care patients; regional health authorities and the provincial health budget.

✔️ Fact-checkVerified: Bill 28, The Health System Governance and Accountability Amendment Act (Nurse-to-Patient Ratios), sponsored by Health Minister Asagwara, received royal assent June 1, 2026 (SM 2026, c. 21) and is described as the first legislation of its kind in Canada. Important clarification: the act enables the minister to ESTABLISH minimum nurse-to-patient ratios by regulation - it does not by itself set specific numeric ratios; as of June 2026 the implementation committee was still being finalized and ratios had not yet been set, so the change will roll out over time.
