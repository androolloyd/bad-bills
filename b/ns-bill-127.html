Bill 127WATCH CLOSELY

Protecting Nova Scotians Act (omnibus)

Sponsor: PC government · Status: Law — Royal Assent Oct 3, 2025 · Nova Scotia · Provincial

A controversial omnibus bill that amends the Crown Lands Act (among other statutes), passed quickly during the fall 2025 sitting.

✅ The case for

Framed by the government as streamlining and protecting Nova Scotians.

⚠️ The case against

Criticized by CBC, the Halifax Examiner and others as a rushed omnibus with resource and rights implications; specific provisions drew pushback.

🔐 Privacy & civil liberties: Limited/uncertain direct privacy angle; the concern is rushed omnibus law-making and Crown-land/resource governance.

👥 Who it affects

Crown-land users and, broadly, the public.

✔️ Fact-checkVerified: Bill 127, the omnibus Protecting Nova Scotians Act, received Royal Assent Oct. 3, 2025 (2025 c.19); it amends seven statutes plus creates one new Act, and notably amends the Crown Lands Act to make it an offence (fines up to $50,000 and/or up to six months in jail) to block or impede forest-access roads or build obstructing structures, while also restricting collection of Social Insurance Numbers and letting gender-based-violence survivors keep their lease terms. Correcting misinformation: critics call the Crown-lands measures an attack on Mi'kmaw land protectors; the government says they target unlawful road blockades, not lawful protest — but the constitutional consultation concerns are real and contested.
