Bill 193LOWER CONCERN

Powering the Economy Act

Sponsor: PC government · Status: Law — April 9, 2026 · Nova Scotia · Provincial

Secures offshore-wind revenue (a levy of $7,000/MW for the first 10 years), shifts grid responsibilities from Nova Scotia Power to a new IESO Nova Scotia, and creates a Subsurface Energy Resource Extraction Act covering geothermal, natural hydrogen, helium and carbon storage.

✅ The case for

Energy modernization, new revenue, and grid-governance reform after Nova Scotia Power's troubles (including a major data breach).

⚠️ The case against

Ratepayer-impact and pace-of-change questions.

🔐 Privacy & civil liberties: Minimal.

👥 Who it affects

Nova Scotia Power ratepayers and the energy sector.

✔️ Fact-checkVerified: Bill 193, the Powering the Economy Act, received Royal Assent April 9, 2026 (2026 c.5); it creates the Offshore Renewable Energy Revenue Act (a royalty/levy regime for offshore wind, about $7,000/MW of installed turbine capacity for the first 10 years), a new Subsurface Energy Resource Extraction Act (geothermal, natural hydrogen, helium, carbon storage and onshore oil and gas), and accelerates the transfer of grid operations from Nova Scotia Power to the Independent Energy System Operator. Correcting expectations: although the bill lets the Minister require utilities to buy offshore-wind power for local use, the government itself expects most offshore wind energy to be exported.
