Bill 198WATCH CLOSELY

Financial Measures (2026) Act

Sponsor: Finance Minister John Lohr (PC) · Status: Law · Nova Scotia · Provincial

Enacts Budget 2026-27 and creates a recurring vehicle levy effective Oct 1, 2026: $500 every two years on electric vehicles and $250 on hybrids.

✅ The case for

Pitched as road-funding fairness as gas-tax revenue falls; Nova Scotia would be the 4th province to do it.

⚠️ The case against

Penalizes cleaner vehicles, adds an affordability hit, and may discourage EV adoption.

🔐 Privacy & civil liberties: Minimal — this is a tax/fiscal measure.

👥 Who it affects

Electric-vehicle and hybrid owners.

✔️ Fact-checkVerified: Bill 198, the Financial Measures (2026) Act implementing the 2026 budget, received Royal Assent April 9, 2026 (2026 c.3) and amends roughly 20 statutes; key measures include new biennial road-use levies on electric ($500) and hybrid ($250) vehicles (from Oct. 1, 2026), raising the financial-institutions capital tax from 4% to 6%, harmonizing the vaping tax with the federal regime, and narrowing preferential forest-property tax rates to active forestry. Correcting a misconception: the budget did NOT change personal or corporate income-tax rates, and the EV charge is a flat registration-style levy, not a per-kilometre road tax.
