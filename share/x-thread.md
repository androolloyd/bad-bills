**2/** First, a myth to kill 🛑

"Bill C-206" is going around as something sinister. It isn't. In this Parliament C-206 is the **National Strategy on Brain Injuries Act** — a harmless health bill.

Bill numbers get recycled every Parliament. Check before you share.

---

**3/** The big one: **C-22, the Lawful Access Act.**

Police & CSIS could demand your subscriber data on "reasonable suspicion" — no warrant — plus pressure on companies to build access into encrypted apps.

Apple warned it would force encryption backdoors. It's in committee, being rushed.

---

**4/** Where C-22 came from: **C-2, the Strong Borders Act.**

After 300+ orgs pushed back, it was split:
• **C-12** (now law): 1-year bar on asylum hearings, power to cancel status, Canada Post can open mail
• **C-22**: the surveillance powers

---

**5/** Two more already passed into law (June 15, 2026):

• **C-8 Cyber Security** — lets government issue *secret* cyber-orders to telecoms
• **C-14 Bail & Sentencing** — tougher reverse-onus bail; lawyers warn it jails more legally-innocent people, hitting Indigenous & Black Canadians hardest

---

**6/** Still moving:

• **C-9 Combatting Hate** — protects communities, but CCLA + labour + faith groups fear vague wording chills protest & speech. Near Royal Assent.
• **C-34 Safe Social Media** — under-16 ban + age-checks for *everyone* = ID/face-scan to use the internet.

---

**7/** And a privacy law that may not protect you: **C-36** rewrites Canada's private-sector privacy rules (PIPEDA).

The CCLA says it actually *erodes* privacy rights and is weak on AI harms. First reading June 15.

**10/** The pattern worth memorizing — how to spot a bad bill:

🚩 omnibus + rushed
🚩 lowers a warrant standard
🚩 exempts government from oversight / FOI
🚩 expands data collection
🚩 retroactive secrecy
🚩 skips consultation
